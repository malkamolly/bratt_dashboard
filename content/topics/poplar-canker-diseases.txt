@welcome
eyebrow: Canker Disease Basics
title: What a canker actually is.
subtitle: A dead, sunken patch in the bark where a fungal infection has killed the cambium.
body: **A canker is a localized area where the fungus has killed the cambium** — the layer of living tissue just beneath the bark. The bark sinks, sometimes cracks, and the tree can't move water and nutrients past that spot.
body: **If a canker rings the stem, it girdles the tree.** That's how cankers kill — by interrupting the vascular system locally, then progressively.
body: **Treatment is limited for both poplar cankers.** This deck is about identification and what to tell the client — not a list of treatments.


@section-divider
number: 01
title: Hypoxylon Canker
tagline: The biggest single cause of poplar death in the Midwest.


@image-side
eyebrow: Hypoxylon Canker
title: The Midwest's poplar killer.
subtitle: A fungal disease that targets stressed poplars and quietly girdles them.
body: **Largest cause of poplar death in the Midwest.** Hypoxylon attacks trees that are already weakened — drought, root damage, construction stress, age.
body: **Look for the bark to slough off** in patches, revealing dark gray or black fungal tissue underneath. Affected limbs die back. Once visible on the trunk, the tree is usually past saving.
body: **No effective fungicide treatment exists.** Management is preventative — reduce stress, water adequately, avoid root damage. Once symptoms show, the conversation is about removal, not cure.

@section-divider
number: 02
title: Septoria Canker
tagline: Less common, equally serious. Can girdle a stem and kill the tree.


@image-side
eyebrow: Septoria Canker
title: A stem-girdling fungus on poplar.
subtitle: Less prevalent than Hypoxylon but just as fatal once established.
body: **Fungal disease that can girdle the stem and kill a poplar.** Septoria primarily attacks **hybrid poplars** but is seen on natives too.
body: **Starts as small dark lesions on twigs and small branches** that expand into sunken cankers. As the canker enlarges, it interrupts the vascular system around it.
body: **Same management approach as Hypoxylon:** reduce stress, water properly, and once a stem is girdled, the affected branch (or tree) is on the removal track.

fit: cover


@three-rules
eyebrow: Talking To Clients
title: The honest poplar conversation.
subtitle: Three things to say when a homeowner asks "can you save this tree?"
rule: Be Straight About The Odds | "By the time we see active canker on the trunk, the tree is usually past treatment. We can talk about how to slow it down — but we should also start thinking about replacement on a timeline."
rule: Stress Is The Trigger | "These fungi are everywhere. They only take down a tree that's already stressed. The best thing we can do for your other poplars is reduce their stress — proper watering, root zone protection, no new construction damage."
rule: Plan The Removal Now, Not Later | "Dead and dying poplars become hazards fast — the wood is brittle and breaks unpredictably. Better to plan a controlled removal than wait for a windstorm to do it for you."
