@section-divider
number: 02
title: How It Works
tagline: Steel cables, bracing rods, and weight reduction — when to use which.


@welcome
eyebrow: Cables
title: What cables actually do.
subtitle: They compress the union, not "hold the tree together."
body: **Steel cables compress branch attachments** and relieve torque from compromised unions. The tree responds by laying down stronger wound-response tissue — the cable is structural, but it's also therapeutic.
body: We use **static steel cables**. Dynamic cabling systems exist (they let the stem travel a bit before catching it), but for a tree that bad we'd usually be doing **weight reduction pruning** anyway.
body: Cables are often paired with weight reduction, but it's not a hard requirement.


@welcome
eyebrow: Bracing Rods
title: Like stitches for the trunk.
subtitle: They close the seam so the tree can heal it shut.
body: Bracing rods **close cracks in stem wood** and prevent future cracking. Wounds that open below branch unions can't be helped by a cable above them — that's a bracing-rod job.
body: This is exactly like getting stitches for a deep cut. **A wound that keeps opening and closing can't compartmentalize.** Hold it shut, and the tree builds wound-response tissue much faster.
body: One rod is rarely enough — see the diagnosis at the end of this deck.


@three-rules
eyebrow: When Not To
title: Trees we don't cable.
subtitle: Cases can be made in most situations, but a few species or situations are a hard no.
rule: Cottonwoods | The wood is too soft and they don't compartmentalize well. Weight reduction is a far better alternative.
rule: Trees Already Failing | A tree in active structural collapse needs to come down, not be patched.
rule: Targets You Can Move | If the only "target" is a fence or a shed the client doesn't care about, sometimes the right answer is to relocate the target, not engineer the tree.

@three-rules
eyebrow: Diagnosis
title: How the work plans out.
subtitle: Three principles that should be in every estimate.
rule: Minimum Two Bracing Rods | A seam goes all the way across the trunk, so it has to be closed in more than one spot to close flat. One rod alone twists the seam open at the far end.
rule: Minimum Two Cables | One stem to another stem with a single cable creates uneven pull on one side. Two cables balance the forces and keep the union under even compression.
rule: Pair With Weight Reduction | Reducing canopy weight at the tip of each codominant stem improves the structural integrity faster than the cable will on its own.
