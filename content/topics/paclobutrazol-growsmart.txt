@welcome
eyebrow: The Mechanism
title: A growth-hormone re-allocation.
subtitle: "What homeowners absolutely do not care to hear about."
body: **Reduced production of gibberellic acid** — the hormone that drives top-growth elongation. **Cell elongation slows, shoots are shorter.**
body: **Increased chlorophyll production** makes for greener leaves. **Increased abscisic acid** reduces water loss; leaves become thicker and waxier.
body: **Improved carbohydrate storage** — energy that would have gone into more canopy is now available for **roots, defense compounds (against insects), and fruit/flower development.** That's where the visible benefits come from.

title: The Research
tagline: Why we trust this product — and what it actually does to roots.


@image-side
eyebrow: Root Density Study
title: Pin oak — treated vs untreated.
subtitle: Gary W. Watson, Journal of Arboriculture, 1996.
body: **Fine root density increased 64% over 3 years** in trees treated with GrowSmart basal injection — compared to untreated controls.
body: **Density increase was greatest in the top 10 cm of soil** — exactly where absorbing roots are most useful and most often damaged by compaction.
body: This is the foundational study that put paclobutrazol on the map as a tree-health treatment, not just a growth regulator for nurseries.

@image-side
eyebrow: GrowSmart vs Soil Trenching
title: Almost as good — at a fraction of the cost.
subtitle: Radial trenching adds 70% root density. GrowSmart adds 64%. Both beat doing nothing.
body: **Radial trenching (SRT) with GrowSmart** produced **70% denser roots** — only **6% better than GrowSmart alone** (64%).
body: **The takeaway:** soil amendment is the clear technical winner. But GrowSmart performs **almost as well** without the expensive, invasive trenching work.
body: For most residential clients, **GrowSmart alone is the right call.** Reserve the full soil-amendment package for high-value trees in very poor soil.
