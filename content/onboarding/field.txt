@steps
eyebrow: Payroll
title: Getting set up in ADP
subtitle: The onboarding admin will enter you into ADP while you download the app.
step: Download ADP Run | While the admin enters your record, install the **ADP Run** app on your phone.
step: Watch for the email | You'll get a welcome email from ADP — open it on your phone.
step: Activate your account | Tap the link in the email and follow the prompts to set your password.
step: Clock in | From this point on, you'll use ADP Run to clock in and out for every shift.

@three-rules
eyebrow: Clocking In & Out
title: Clock in and out at the shop.
subtitle: The system enforces this — you can't punch from anywhere else.
rule: Be at the shop by 7:15 AM | Unless your manager has told you otherwise.
rule: Clock in promptly at 7:15 AM | Use ADP Run to start your shift when you arrive.
rule: Clock out before you leave | At the end of your shift, clock out before leaving the shop. If you forget, fill out the **time-punch correction** form in Slack.


@welcome
eyebrow: Safety
title: Safety Meetings — 2 days a week.
subtitle: Usually Mondays and Wednesdays, 7:15 AM.
body: We work in a profession where things can go wrong fast. Twice a week we sit down together to talk about specific risks, recent incidents, and what we're doing to stay safe.
body: Show up on time and engaged. These meetings exist because they save people from getting hurt.

title: Time off and calling out — use Slack.
subtitle: We get it: things come up.

@welcome
eyebrow: Repairs
title: Something break down? Slack it.
subtitle: We have a workflow for that — don't try to fix it yourself.
body: Anytime a piece of equipment isn't working right, use the appropriate **repair workflow in Slack**. This tells the team what's wrong and gets the item into the queue.
body: Put the broken item where the team expects to find it, and let the workflow do the rest. We'll repair it and get it back to your crew.


@welcome
eyebrow: Incident Reports
title: Any damage — fill it out.
subtitle: Every time, no matter how small.
body: Anytime there is **any type of damage to anything**, an incident report is **required**. You'll find the form in Slack.
body: Include all the details — **names, times, photos**. The more we know, the better we can prevent it from happening again. Our goal is to reduce incidents to zero.
