@section-divider

title: Why We Care
tagline: It lives in the soil, kills trees, and there is no cure.


@three-rules
eyebrow: The Headline
title: Three things to know
subtitle: Internalize these before anything else.
rule: It Kills | **Verticillium wilt kills trees.** Once a tree is infected the prognosis is grim — and there is no treatment currently available.
rule: It Hides | The fungus lives in the soil for years with no visible evidence, then infects roots and gets pulled up into the canopy.
rule: It's Common Here | Sugar maple, catalpa, and magnolia get hit hardest in our area. Sugar maple is especially common on the **west side** of the Twin Cities.


@welcome
eyebrow: The Mechanism
title: It attacks the xylem.
subtitle: A water-translocation issue, not a leaf issue.
body: The **xylem** is the plumbing inside a tree — the tissue that pulls water from the roots up into the canopy. Verticillium wilt **plugs the xylem**, so water can't get where it needs to go.
body: When water flow is diminished or cut off, the leaves start to **dry out (desiccate)**. That's why you see tip dieback and wilting throughout the canopy even when the weather has been wet.
body: This is also why fungicide doesn't help — the fungus isn't on the leaves, it's clogging the pipes from the inside.


@section-divider
number: 02
title: Identifying It
tagline: What it looks like in the canopy, in the leaves, and under the bark.


@welcome
eyebrow: Symptoms
title: What to look for.
subtitle: No single symptom guarantees a diagnosis — look for the pattern.
body: **Abnormal leaf color**, wilting throughout the canopy, **small leaf size**, thinning canopy with or without other obvious adverse conditions, and **tip dieback** are all common.
body: **Streaks under the bark** on twigs are a strong tell, but not necessary for ID. Maples streak **green**, catalpas streak **brown**. You may or may not see them.
body: A **lab test** is the only way to guarantee a diagnosis. If a client wants certainty, that's the path.

@section-divider
number: 03
title: Living With It
tagline: How to advise clients when their soil already has it.


@image-side
eyebrow: Where it lives
title: It lives in the soil — for years.
subtitle: You can't remove it. You can only replace what you plant.
body: The fungus stays in the soil and **cannot be removed** by treatment, fumigation, or replacement of the topsoil at any practical scale. It can sit dormant for years with no visible evidence.
body: When a susceptible tree's roots hit infected soil, the fungal bodies enter the roots and travel up into the canopy.
body: The path forward is **removal + replacement with an unsusceptible species** — not retreating the existing tree.

@three-rules
eyebrow: How to Talk to Clients
title: Lead with honesty.
subtitle: Three moves that work for this conversation.
rule: Name It Clearly | Use the words "Verticillium wilt" and explain it in plain language: a soil fungus that plugs the tree's plumbing.
rule: Set Expectations | There is **no cure**. Treatment isn't the answer. The honest path is removal and replacement with a species that isn't susceptible.
rule: Offer the Path Forward | Bring the resistant-species list to the conversation. Clients want a plan, not just bad news.
