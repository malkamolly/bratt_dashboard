@section-divider
number: 02
title: Fuel & Equipment
tagline: How to refuel, where to find tools, and how to get repairs done.


@welcome
eyebrow: FuelCloud *(future)*
title: How we manage refueling.
subtitle: A smart fuel system that tracks who's using what.
body: Bratt Tree uses **FuelCloud** to manage our refueling. You'll use the app to tell the system **who you are** and **which item you're refueling** — a truck, a saw, a piece of equipment.
body: This matters because Minnesota has different tax rates for **on-road** and **off-road** diesel. FuelCloud lets us track this accurately and keep us compliant.


@steps
eyebrow: FuelCloud Setup *(future)*
title: Getting you into FuelCloud
subtitle: Give the onboarding admin this info to get set up.
step: Share your info | First name, last name, email, and cell phone number.
step: Pick a 5-digit PIN | Choose a number unique to you — you'll use it every time you refuel.
step: Admin enters you | They'll confirm your PIN isn't already taken and add you to the system.
step: We'll show you in person | During your tour, someone will walk you through actually using the system.


@welcome
eyebrow: Fill Station
title: The Bratt Tree Fill Station
subtitle: Set up like a gas station — for trucks, equipment, and saws.
body: The Fill Station was built to make truck and equipment servicing **easy and efficient**. You're expected to **fill all your trucks and equipment at the end of each shift** — don't leave it for the morning.
body: Treat the space like you would a public gas station. Remove trash and debris from your truck daily, and use the dumpster at the Fill Station. We're always working to improve this space.

@checklist
eyebrow: Saws
title: Chain saw items
subtitle: Where chains live, and how to handle repairs.
- **Sharp chains** are in the garage next to the shop — grab sharp ones, hang dull ones back up
- Our team uses an **automatic chain sharpener** for precision — **no field sharpening**
- **Repairs** — submit via Slack using the repair request workflow
- **Shed** — set the broken item in the shed for the team to pick up
- The team will repair it and return it to your crew
- **Don't attempt repairs yourself** — we have backup saws available

@three-rules
eyebrow: Clocking In & Out
title: Clock in and out at the shop.
subtitle: The system enforces this — you can't punch from anywhere else.
rule: Be at the shop by 7:15 AM | Unless your manager has told you otherwise.
rule: Clock in promptly at 7:15 AM | Use ADP Run to start your shift when you arrive.
rule: Clock out before you leave | At the end of your shift, clock out before leaving the shop. If you forget, fill out the **time-punch correction** form in Slack.


@welcome
eyebrow: Safety
title: Safety Meetings — 2 days a week.
subtitle: Usually Mondays and Wednesdays, 7:15 AM.
body: We work in a profession where things can go wrong fast. Twice a week we sit down together to talk about specific risks, recent incidents, and what we're doing to stay safe.
body: Show up on time and engaged. These meetings exist because they save people from getting hurt.

title: Time off and calling out — use Slack.

@welcome
eyebrow: Small Engine Repairs
title: Saw broken? Leaf blower won't start?
subtitle: Use the Slack workflow — don't try to fix it yourself.
body: When a saw or leaf blower breaks down, fill out the **small engine repair** workflow in Slack. This tells the team what's wrong and gets the item into the repair queue.
body: Put the broken item in the **Shed** so the team can find it. We'll repair it and get it back to your crew.


@welcome
eyebrow: Incident Reports
title: Any damage — fill it out.
subtitle: Every time, no matter how small.
body: Anytime there is **any type of damage to anything**, an incident report is **required**. You'll find the form in Slack.
body: Include all the details — **names, times, photos**. The more we know, the better we can prevent it from happening again. Our goal is to reduce incidents to zero.
