    ## What is it?

    Oak wilt is a fungal disease that kills oaks fast &mdash; especially red oaks, which can die within weeks of showing symptoms. It is one of the most serious tree diseases in the eastern United States.

    ## How to spot it

    Look for these signs on a declining oak:

    - Leaves wilt and brown from the **edges inward**
    - Browning progresses from the **top of the canopy downward**
    - Leaves drop while still **partly green** &mdash; not fully brown like drought stress

    ![Oak wilt canopy decline &mdash; note browning beginning at top left of crown](/assets/img/meetings/oak-wilt-canopy-decline.jpg)

    ## How it spreads

    Oak wilt spreads two ways:

    - **Sap beetles** carry fungal spores to fresh pruning wounds or broken branches, primarily in spring
    - **Root grafts** between neighboring oaks allow the fungus to travel underground from an infected tree to healthy ones nearby

    ## How to manage it

    - **No pruning April through July** &mdash; open wounds attract sap beetles during peak flight season
    - **Trench** between infected and healthy trees to sever root grafts and stop underground spread
    - **Remove and burn** (or chip and bury) infected wood &mdash; do not leave it on site or chip it onto the lawn

    ## Sales talking points

    - Lead with **prevention timing** &mdash; spring is the highest-risk window; clients who act now avoid a much bigger problem
    - **Trenching** is a concrete protective service you can offer for oaks growing near a confirmed infected tree
    - When you see decline starting on one side of the canopy, treat it as urgent &mdash; red oaks do not recover once infected
